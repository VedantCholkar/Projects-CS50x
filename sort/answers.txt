sort1 uses: Bubble sort

How do you know?: It is slow on random lists but significantly faster on sorted lists. It has a O(n2) and Ω(n) which matchs the test results.

sort2 uses: Merge sort

How do you know?: It is faster than both the sorts on random lists and results of random and sorted lists matched almost exactly. It has a Θ(n log n) which matches test results.

sort3 uses: Selection sort

How do you know?: It performs almost exactly the same on both random and sorted lists. It has Θ(n2).
